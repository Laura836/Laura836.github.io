History

This recipe originates from Puebla, Mexico where many other famous dishes were created. The recipe consists of mainly shredded chicken and a tostada. The tostado was made in Oaxaca, Mexico. When tortillas lost their freshness and became too stiff to fold they didn't want to waste them so they ate them hard, to then call them tostadas. They got the name from what they were. Tostada translates to toasted.

Ingredients

  • 7 ounces chipotle peppers in adobo
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 pachet of chicken flavoring
  • 3 tomatoes
  • black pepper
  • white onion
  • 3 chicken breasts
  • laurel leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • cheese
  • lettuce
  • sour cream
  • tostadas

Directions

  1. Boil pot of water and add half an onion, 2 laurel leaves, tomatoes, garlic, chicken, and salt into water.
  2. Once chicken is cooked, take it out and set aside. The rest that's in the pot add into a blender. Of the broth add 1/2 a cup. Also add packet of chicken flavoring and chipotle peppers. Blend
  3. Shred the chicken and set aside. In another pan, stir-fry the other half of the onion until golden brown and add the salsa. Once the salsa boils add shredded chicken.
  4. Prepare toppings. Cut the lettuce and shred cheese if necessary
  5. Spread sour cream onto the tostada, add the tinga, and finally the lettuce and cheese
